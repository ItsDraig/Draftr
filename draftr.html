How the Algorithm Works v1.0
▼
Scoring Formula 3 components
Each team receives a score from 0–100 based on three weighted components. The final score maps to a letter grade, and the higher-scoring team is marked as favored.
Score =
  Coherence  × 0.50
+ Threat      × 0.35
+ Dmg Balance × 0.15
The verdict edge label — EVEN, SLIGHT EDGE, or CLEAR EDGE — reflects the raw score gap between the two teams (<5, <15, or 15+).
Component Breakdown weights
Coherence (50%) — measures how cleanly your five picks align to a single playstyle. Each champion carries one or more archetype tags. The most common tag across the team determines the label; the proportion of champions contributing to it drives this score. A pure Dive comp scores higher than a scattered one.
Threat (35%) — the average individual threat rating of your five champions, normalized to 0–100. Ratings range from 6 (low-threat utility picks) to 9 (high-carry assassins and hyperscalers).
Damage Balance (15%) — a flat bonus awarded when your composition has at least 25% of both physical and magic damage. Itemizing against a one-type team is far easier for opponents.

Champions can carry multiple archetype tags. A comp's label is the plurality winner — the archetype with the most contributors. Ties resolve to the first alphabetically.
Champion data is sourced from Riot's Data Dragon CDN. Archetype tags, threat ratings, and damage classifications are manually curated and updated over time. Champions not present in the database default to neutral values (threat 6, 50/50 damage, no archetype contribution) and do not break scoring — they simply contribute less signal. Data Dragon version is fetched live on each API connection.
